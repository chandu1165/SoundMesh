# Vocal Clarity Reference

Vocal clarity depends on a chain of decisions, not one EQ boost. First match the vocal level against the instrumental. Next remove masking from guitars, keys, pads, and reverbs before boosting the vocal itself.

Common clarity areas are 2 kHz to 5 kHz. Use small boosts carefully because this range can become sharp. If consonants hurt, use dynamic EQ or de-essing around 5 kHz to 8 kHz instead of lowering the whole vocal.

Low-mid buildup around 180 Hz to 350 Hz can hide words. Prefer cutting instruments that compete with the vocal before cutting the vocal body too much.
